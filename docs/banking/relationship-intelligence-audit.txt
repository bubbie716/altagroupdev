Relationship Intelligence — Production Readiness Audit
======================================================

Audit-only review of personal RI (V1–V4), company RI, integrations, security, and
documentation. Findings are categorized by severity with recommended fixes.

Date: 2025 (audit session)
Scope: Personal + Company Relationship Intelligence, scoring, timeline,
recommendations, product integrations, admin/customer UI, services, models, docs,
permissions, schedulers, audit logging.


EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------

Relationship Intelligence is a strong foundation with clear read-only posture,
operator tooling, V4 product integrations, and separate company profiles. It is
NOT production-ready as a core banking system until critical calculation bugs,
staleness, and scheduler gaps are resolved.

Strengths:
- Layered operator auth, customer sanitization (no V2 scores/factors)
- Recommendation prefill without auto-apply
- Company aggregation scoped to companyId
- Extensive documentation

Blockers:
- Personal overdue penalties never apply (cap() bug)
- Alta Pay double-counting
- Broken asset-milestone backfill
- Unwired schedulers
- Profiles go stale without manual refresh
- Dual legacy (0–100) vs V2 (0–1000) scoring on Alta Card pages


================================================================================
CRITICAL
================================================================================

C1 — Personal overdue installment penalties never apply
Severity: Critical
Location: src/server/relationship-intelligence.service.ts — cap() (L52–54),
          computeScoreFromInputs() (L525–537)

Personal cap() floors negatives at zero: Math.min(Math.max(value, 0), max).
Overdue impact is negative (-25 per installment), so cap(-25, 100) → 0 and score
never decreases for overdue installments despite factors UI showing a penalty.

Company cap() handles negatives correctly — behavior diverges.

Why it matters: Delinquent borrowers can retain inflated scores and tiers,
affecting lending, card, and private eligibility.

Recommended fix: Align personal cap() with company logic, or apply negative caps
without flooring at zero. Add tests for multi-overdue scenarios.


C2 — Alta Pay volume double-counted (personal)
Severity: Critical
Location: src/server/relationship-intelligence.service.ts —
          aggregateRelationshipInputs() (L149–167, L284–287)

Two aggregations are summed:
1. All approved txns with "Alta Pay" on personal + member-company accounts
2. DEPOSIT txns with "Alta Pay" on company accounts

Company Alta Pay deposits match both → inflated lifetimeAltaPayVolume, score
(up to +100 Alta Pay cap), and private-banking signals.

Why it matters: Business-heavy users get materially overstated relationship
strength on personal profiles.

Recommended fix: Use a single scoped query, or exclude DEPOSITs already counted
in the sent aggregate.


C3 — Asset milestone backfill throws at runtime
Severity: Critical
Location: src/server/relationship-timeline.service.ts —
          backfillAssetMilestonesFromSnapshots() (L413)

References undefined profileId inside the function. Asset milestone backfill
fails when invoked.

Why it matters: Admin "Backfill timeline" partially fails; asset milestones never
populate.

Recommended fix: Resolve profileId via resolveProfileId(userId) like other
backfill paths.


C4 — No automated profile refresh in production
Severity: Critical (operational)
Location: Schedulers exist but no cron routes; no product-event hooks

- relationship-intelligence-scheduler.service.ts and
  relationship-intelligence-recommendation-scheduler.service.ts are NOT wired to
  /api/cron/* (only billing, statements, loan-interest, transfers exist).
- No refreshRelationshipProfile calls from bank.service, loan.service,
  lending.service, or alta-card services.
- Customer /bank/relationship reads persisted profiles → can show stale
  tier/assets indefinitely.

Why it matters: Core banking intelligence that doesn't update is misleading for
operators and customers.

Recommended fix: Register cron routes; add best-effort refresh on high-signal
events (loan funded, card opened, large deposit); document SLA if cron-only.


================================================================================
HIGH
================================================================================

H1 — Personal RI intentionally mixes all member company data
Severity: High
Location: relationship-intelligence.service.ts — aggregateRelationshipInputs()

Any companyMembership pulls company bank balances, loans, cards, Alta Pay, and
product bonuses into the personal score. Docs state company profiles are
independent; personal profiles still include company data. Multi-company members
stack assets; minor members inherit full company financials.

Why it matters: Conflicts with data-isolation intent; same assets scored on
company profile AND every member's personal profile.

Recommended fix: Product decision: (A) narrow personal aggregation to
{ userId, companyId: null } only, or (B) document dual-counting and cap company
contribution. Do not ship pre-approvals until decided.


H2 — Dual scoring systems on Alta Card internal pages
Severity: High
Location: Legacy alta-card-relationship-pricing.service.ts (0–100) vs V2
          relationship-intelligence.service.ts (0–1000)

Same pages show both:
- /internal/alta-card/applications/$applicationId
- /internal/alta-card/reviews/$reviewId
- /internal/alta-card/$cardId

Operators see two different scores with no explanation.

Why it matters: Underwriting decisions based on wrong or conflicting signals.

Recommended fix: Converge or clearly label legacy vs V2; remove legacy block
once V2 is authoritative.


H3 — Company active loan balance likely double-counts
Severity: High
Location: company-relationship-intelligence.service.ts (L220–228)

Sums outstandingBalance + accruedInterest + principalOutstanding. Personal path
uses outstandingBalance + accruedInterest only. If outstandingBalance is total
payoff, adding principalOutstanding overstates exposure and currentCreditExposure.

Why it matters: Wrong commercial lending and card limit context.

Recommended fix: Align with personal loan balance logic; verify against
loan.service.ts field semantics.


H4 — Company refresh blocked from automation
Severity: High
Location: refreshCompanyRelationshipProfile() calls requireOperator()

refreshAllCompanyRelationshipProfiles() cannot run from cron without an operator
session. No company scheduler service exists despite
COMPANY_RELATIONSHIP_INTELLIGENCE_JOB_KEY in config.

Why it matters: Company RI stays manual-only; verified companies never get
profiles unless an operator acts.

Recommended fix: System-actor batch refresh (mirror personal); add company
scheduler + cron route.


H5 — Company refresh missing post-refresh side effects
Severity: High
Location: company-relationship-intelligence.service.ts

Personal refreshRelationshipProfile() triggers recommendation regeneration +
timeline sync. Company refresh does neither — recommendations only via manual
"Regenerate" in UI.

Why it matters: Company recommendations and timeline drift from profile state.

Recommended fix: Hook generateCompanyRelationshipRecommendations and timeline
sync after company refresh (best-effort).


H6 — Invalid tier code in company recommendation config
Severity: High (latent)
Location: company-relationship-recommendation-config.ts —
          recommendedBusinessCardTierFromRelationship() returns "standard"

AltaCardTierCode is white | navy | black | gold. Dead code today but unsafe if
wired.

Recommended fix: Return "white" or remove until used.


H7 — Company timeline audit uses company ID as actor
Severity: High (audit integrity)
Location: company-relationship-timeline.service.ts —
          createCompanyRelationshipTimelineEvent()

actorUserId: input.companyId for COMPANY_RELATIONSHIP_TIMELINE_EVENT_CREATED.

Why it matters: Invalid audit attribution; breaks actor-based reporting.

Recommended fix: Use system actor or authenticated user ID.


H8 — Company customer timeline lacks positive-only filter
Severity: High (security/privacy)
Location: company-relationship-timeline.service.ts vs personal
          CUSTOMER_VISIBLE_TIMELINE_EVENT_TYPES

Company customer view only hides MANUAL_NOTE. Personal timeline has a rich
allowlist. Future negative event types (score changes, denials) would leak to
company members.

Recommended fix: Mirror personal allowlist pattern for company customer timeline.


================================================================================
MEDIUM
================================================================================

M1 — Recommendations expire on every profile refresh
Severity: Medium
Location: relationship-intelligence-recommendation.service.ts (L478–481)

All ACTIVE recommendations → EXPIRED on regenerate. Breaks in-progress admin
review and deep links.

Recommended fix: Expire only superseded types, or mark stale without invalidating
in-review rows.


M2 — Double recommendation generation on first profile
Severity: Medium
Location: generateRelationshipRecommendations() calls refreshRelationshipProfile()
when no profile; refresh already calls generateRelationshipRecommendations().

Recommended fix: Guard with skipRecommendations flag on refresh, or skip nested
call in generate.


M3 — Accept/deep-link prefill skips RELATIONSHIP_RECOMMENDATION_USED audit
Severity: Medium
Location: V4 integration vs relationship-recommendations-panel.tsx Accept flow

"Use Recommendation" audits correctly; Accept + URL params prefill ops form
without USED audit.

Recommended fix: Unify prefill paths through useRelationshipRecommendationRecord,
or audit Accept prefill explicitly.


M4 — Personal card recommendations ignore business-only cardholders
Severity: Medium
Location: resolvePrimaryAltaCard() filters ownerUserId: userId only

Users with only company business cards get no tier/limit/rate recommendations
despite aggregated profile including business card data.

Recommended fix: Separate personal vs business recommendation contexts, or
resolve company card for business users.


M5 — Recommendations can diverge from persisted profile
Severity: Medium
Location: generateRelationshipRecommendations() uses live calculateRelationshipProfile()
while stored score/tier may lag.

Recommended fix: Refresh before generate, or generate from persisted snapshot with
explicit "live preview" label.


M6 — Live timeline dedupe blocks repeat tier/limit changes
Severity: Medium
Location: relationship-timeline.service.ts — hasEntityEvent()

Same eventType + relatedEntityId blocks second tier change on same card.

Recommended fix: Include timestamp or change metadata in dedupe key instead of
entity-only dedupe.


M7 — Live vs backfill score/tier dedupe key mismatch
Severity: Medium
Location: Backfill uses score:${snapshot.id}; live sync uses score:value:${newScore}

Can produce duplicate score/tier events.

Recommended fix: Standardize dedupe keys.


M8 — Company timeline has no live product hooks
Severity: Medium
Location: Company timeline service only; bank.service writes personal timeline on
business account open

Business account opens appear on owner personal timeline, not company timeline.

Recommended fix: Emit company timeline events when companyId is set on product
mutations.


M9 — /bank/relationship not in bank navigation
Severity: Medium (product completeness)
Location: bank-sub-nav.tsx — no link; route exists at /bank/relationship

Recommended fix: Add nav entry or cross-link from dashboard/products.


M10 — Orphaned code / missing routes
Severity: Medium
- fetchCompanyRelationshipIntelligenceDashboard — no UI route
- RelationshipRecommendationsWidget — never imported
- No company relationship queue on /internal/companies

Recommended fix: Wire or remove dead code.


M11 — Defense-in-depth gaps on service layer
Severity: Medium
- getCustomerCompanyRelationshipTimeline() — no auth in service (only server fn)
- refreshRelationshipProfile / generateRelationshipRecommendations — no
  requireOperator in service
- fetchRelationshipProfileSummary in bank namespace — callable by any authed user
  until service rejects

Recommended fix: Enforce auth at service boundary for all entry points.


M12 — Unbounded snapshot growth
Severity: Medium (performance/ops)
Every profile refresh creates a snapshot with no retention policy (personal +
company).

Recommended fix: Retention policy or snapshot-on-change-only.


M13 — Failed autopay / delinquency penalties inconsistent
Severity: Medium
Personal: flat -40 regardless of count. Company: per-card. Neither filters
closed cards with stale autopayLastStatus: FAILED. Delinquent/default penalties
flat regardless of count.

Recommended fix: Align penalty models; filter by active card status.


M14 — totalInvestments hardcoded to 0
Severity: Medium (documented placeholder)
Exchange/Terminal not integrated; totalAltaAssets = bank + 0.

Recommended fix: Accept for now; ensure UI/docs say "bank assets" where
investments are implied.


M15 — Pre-approval readiness vs recommendation path divergence
Severity: Medium
getPreApprovalReadiness() uses rule logic; does not read persisted
LOAN_PRE_APPROVAL recommendation. Paths can disagree.

Recommended fix: Single source of truth or explicit "readiness ≠ offer" copy.
