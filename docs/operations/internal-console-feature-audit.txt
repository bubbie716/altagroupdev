Alta Internal Console — Product Feature Audit
==============================================

Assumption: The console is stable and functionally correct. This evaluates
operational completeness for a digital bank, not bugs or UI polish.

Method: Review of routes, workspaces, queues, server capabilities, and docs
against what banking ops, compliance, support, and risk teams typically need
on day one.


PERSPECTIVE SUMMARY
-------------------

Role                          | Verdict
------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------
Head of Banking Operations    | Core money-movement queues and account lifecycle
                              | are solid. Missing workload management, SLA
                              | visibility, historical reporting, and bulk deny.
COO                           | Can run daily ops at modest scale. Cannot run a
                              | full compliance/fraud program or management
                              | reporting from the console alone.
Operations Manager            | Operators can complete most tasks, but often via
                              | extra clicks (queue → workspace, no approve-from-
                              | transaction). No "my queue" or assignment.
Compliance Officer            | Audit trail + export is good. No case workflow,
                              | investigation narrative, or regulatory reporting
                              | — signals only.
Customer Support Lead         | Customer 360 + notes + cross-links work. No
                              | unified case/ticket, dispute tracking, or customer
                              | comms log outside deal rooms.
Fraud/Risk Lead               | Largest gap. No fraud queue, flags, watchlists,
                              | card disputes, or velocity controls in-console.


AREA-BY-AREA ASSESSMENT
-----------------------

DASHBOARD
Can operators work efficiently? Partially — good situational awareness (queue
depths, health, activity feed).

Missing: My assignments, overdue/aging items, fraud/compliance case counts,
shift handoff summary.

Extra clicks: Activity feed is read-only; operators must jump to queues manually.

Expected but absent: Queue aging heatmap, "items waiting >24h," operator on-call
roster.


QUEUES
Strong: Deposits, withdrawals (approve/deny + bulk approve), account openings,
company verification, lending/Alta Card apps & reviews, deal room inbox,
exceptions triage, private banking list.

Gaps:
- Bulk deny (server exists, UI does not)
- Approve/deny from transaction workspace
- Queue aging/SLA columns
- Assignment (who owns this item)
- Export queue results
- Exceptions queue is read-only (no resolve/dismiss/assign)


CUSTOMER / COMPANY / ACCOUNT WORKSPACES
Strong: Tabbed 360, notes, audit, related records, holds/restrictions (account),
tags/status (customer), company verify/reject, RI on customer/company.

Gaps:
- No KYC document review vault
- No customer-level restriction (account-level only)
- No case attached to customer
- Company workspace doesn't deep-link business Alta Card ops inline


TRANSACTION WORKSPACE
Strong: Read-only detail, audit, notes, cross-links.

Gap (important): No approve/deny/review actions — operators must find the item
in deposit/withdrawal queues. Real banks expect transaction-centric review for
escalations.


ALTA CARD WORKSPACE + CARDS LIST
Strong: Full card ops (status, limits, tier, payments, fees, adjustments,
employee cards, autopay), cards browse list, notes via cardholder.

Gaps: No disputes/chargebacks, fraud blocks, card-specific notes (uses USER
target), no export.


LOAN WORKSPACE
Strong: Servicing actions (interest, freeze, waive, payoff, adjustment, manual
payment), schedule, notes, audit.

Gaps: No collections workflow, restructuring, write-off beyond interest waive;
deal room tab is link-only from loan (no thread from active loan).


SECURE DEAL ROOMS
Strong: Thread-based deal rooms for lending + Alta Card (message, attachments,
status, decision panels in workspaces).

Gaps: No full negotiation workflow (offers, document checklist, officer
assignment) in UI — backend DealRoom model exists but console uses application
threads only. No SLA visibility.


RELATIONSHIP INTELLIGENCE
Strong: Score/tier, holdings, recommendations with prefill into underwriting,
private banking signals, timeline.

Gaps: Not a CRM — no manual tier override audit, recommendation dismiss/escalate,
or company RI index page. Acceptable for V1 credit desk.


GLOBAL SEARCH
Strong: Cross-entity search (customers, accounts, txs, loans, cards, apps,
audit refs, jobs).

Gaps: No advanced filters, saved searches, or bulk actions from results. Fine
for V1.


JOBS
Strong: Catalog, last run health, admin manual run + audit.

Gaps: No per-run history drill-down, failed-item retry, or alerting config.


AUDIT
Strong: Filters, entity deep-links from workspaces, CSV export (5k rows).

Gaps: No scheduled export, case attachment, retention/certification UI. Strong
for V1.


REPORTS
Present: Today-only totals with drill-down to queues/transactions/audit.

Gaps: No historical reporting, trends, regulatory extracts, or CSV export.
COO/compliance will expect more for V1 bank ops.


COMPLIANCE
Present: Live risk signal cards with deep links.

Gaps: Explicitly no case management. No fraud monitoring, watchlists, SAR
workflow. Signals ≠ investigations.


SETTINGS
Present: Maintenance mode (admin), live ops status summary.

Gaps: No role/permission UI, queue SLAs, notification routing, operator roster.

1. MISSING CRITICAL OPERATOR CAPABILITIES
-----------------------------------------

1. Structured case management (assign, status, resolution, link to entities)
2. Card dispute / chargeback intake and tracking (if Alta Card is production)
3. Fraud / suspicious-activity flagging on customers, accounts, transactions
4. Queue aging and SLA visibility across money-movement and application queues
5. Historical reporting beyond "today" with export
6. Transaction-level review actions outside deposit/withdrawal queues
7. Exceptions resolution workflow (not just deep links)


2. MISSING QUALITY-OF-LIFE IMPROVEMENTS
---------------------------------------

1. Bulk deny for deposits/withdrawals (backend ready)
2. CSV export from queues and explore lists
3. Approve/deny lending or Alta Card applications from queue (without opening
   workspace)
4. Job run failure drill-down
5. Card-specific internal notes (today: cardholder USER notes only)
6. Loan workspace → linked deal room thread when origination thread exists
7. "My open items" filter on deal room inbox
8. Audit metadata filters (source/severity) once standardized

4. FEATURES THAT CAN WAIT UNTIL V2
----------------------------------

- Operator assignment and "my queue" across all work types
- Full KYC document vault with versioning
- Collections cases for loans and delinquent cards
- Watchlists and enhanced monitoring programs
- SAR/regulatory filing workflow
- Role/permission self-service admin
- Proactive ops alerting (email/Slack on SLA breach)
- Full DealRoom negotiation UI (offers, counters, checklist) if thread model
  stays sufficient
- Customer support ticket system integrated with comms log
- Company-level relationship intelligence index page
- Saved searches and advanced search filters


5. OVERALL SCORE: 72 / 100
--------------------------

Breakdown (approximate):
- Core banking ops (deposits, withdrawals, accounts, holds, adjustments): 85
- Credit ops (lending, Alta Card, RI, deal rooms): 80
- Audit & jobs: 78
- Reporting & compliance program: 45
- Fraud / disputes / case management: 25
- Operator productivity (assignment, SLA, bulk, exports): 60

The console is strong for a focused digital bank with deposits, lending, and
cards. It is not yet complete for a regulated institution that must run fraud,
disputes, and compliance investigations entirely inside ops tooling.


6. IS THE INTERNAL CONSOLE PRODUCTION-READY?
--------------------------------------------

Conditionally yes — for a controlled launch with:
- Low-to-moderate transaction volume
- Manual fraud/dispute handling outside the console (spreadsheet + audit log)
- Compliance investigations documented in notes + external case tool
- Ops team small enough that assignment is informal

Not production-ready as a self-contained ops platform for a bank that must prove
end-to-end investigation, dispute, and reporting capability to regulators or
card networks without external tools.


7. WOULD YOU OPERATE THIS BANK EVERY DAY?
-----------------------------------------

Yes, with reservations.

Comfortable because:
- Money-movement review is first-class (queues, reason, audit, bulk approve)
- Customer/account 360 supports support and ops investigations
- Underwriting (lending + Alta Card) with secure threads and RI is above average
  for a V1
- Audit export and job visibility reduce "what happened?" uncertainty
- Maintenance mode and admin/operator separation exist

Uncomfortable because:
- I would run disputes and compliance cases in a parallel system
- I could not answer "show me all items older than 48 hours" from the console
  alone
- Fraud escalation would be ad hoc (notes + tags, no formal flag workflow)
- Management reporting would require pulling audit CSVs or DB reports, not
  /internal/reports

For Alta's stated V1 scope (internal digital bank, credit desk model, no full
AML program yet), this is operable. For "we are a full retail/card bank," I would
want case management, disputes, and aging on the critical path before calling
V1 complete.

BOTTOM LINE
-----------

Alta Internal Console is a credible V1 operations platform for core banking
and credit products. The highest-value gap is not more dashboards — it is
closing the loop on exceptions, disputes, and investigations with cases, aging,
and historical reporting, so operators never need to leave the console to answer
"what's open, who owns it, and what did we decide?"
