================================================================================
2. TOP STRENGTHS
================================================================================

1. Centralized audit architecture — Every meaningful operator action flows
   through writeAuditLog() → AuditLog → optional staff Discord channel. Money
   movement, company verification, lending/card decisions, deal room ops, and
   internal notes are covered.

2. Consistent operator action pattern — OpsConfirmDialog requires a reason for
   most money-moving actions; silent customer notification is explicit, logged,
   and defaults to notify. This is the right institutional instinct.

3. Status-guarded approvals — Deposits, withdrawals, loan/card applications,
   and company verification reject double-approve/double-deny at the status
   level. Bulk ops exist with aggregate impact preview.

4. Correction-by-offset model — Manual credits/debits, Alta Pay reversals, Alta
   Card transaction reversals, and account reopen create new ledger entries
   rather than mutating history. Audit trail stays court-readable.

5. Credit Desk institutional controls — Admin-only desk open/close; closing
   bulk-cancels pending applications with system messages and thread closure.
   Real bank behavior.

6. Internal notes are append-only and audited — INTERNAL_NOTE_ADDED on every
   note; no silent deletion. Good for investigations.

7. Tag management has real guardrails — Last-admin protection, self-admin
   lockout, operator cannot grant/revoke staff tags. Alta Private invitations
   are admin-only.

8. V1 performance hardening — Deduped loaders, debounced search, scoped 360
   views. Shows ops scalability was considered.

15. FINAL LAUNCH READINESS ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

Can Alta launch as a premium digital financial institution today?

Controlled soft launch: Yes, with a small ops team (<10 staff), <500 active
customers, and explicit runbook acknowledgment of known gaps.

Full institution launch at stated scale (10k customers, 1k companies, 100
staff): No.

What works today

Alta behaves like a real bank in the ways that matter most for trust: money
doesn't move on deposit/withdraw until an operator approves; every significant
action leaves an audit row; operator actions require reasons in the console;
corrections create offsetting ledger entries; Credit Desk open/close is
institutional; internal notes are append-only and attributed.

An experienced ops lead could run Alta today with written procedures
compensating for missing dual control and manual recovery paths.

What would embarrass Alta under scrutiny

- An operator approves three pending withdrawals that collectively exceed
  available balance
- Alta Pay reversed twice, creating duplicate credits
- Interbank transfer sits in PENDING_REVIEW forever with no ops path
- Audit row missing after a successful transfer due to non-atomic audit write
- Deal room reopened on web but customer can't use Discord channel
- Compliance asks "show me all failed transfer attempts" — nothing exists

Brutally honest summary

Alta's operational philosophy is correct: accountability, auditability, reason
capture, and correction-by-offset are built into the architecture. The internal
console is functionally complete for V1 banking ops — queues, workspaces, bulk
actions, exceptions disposition, and audit export exist.

The gaps are not missing buttons. They are financial integrity defects
(concurrency, withdrawal approval math), missing control depth (single-operator
money creation, no dual approval), and incomplete recovery/linkage (Alta Pay
double-reverse, no deposit/withdrawal undo, adjustment reverse without UI).

For a Minecraft institution aiming at premium realism: you are 70% of the way to
"real bank ops" and 50% of the way to "safe at scale." Fix P0 before marketing
Alta as institution-grade. Fix P1 before scaling past a few hundred customers
or more than ~15 concurrent operators.

AUDIT AREA SUMMARIES (SECTIONS 1–12)
================================================================================

1. Administrative Permissions — Two-tier model (admin/operator) with thin
   separation. Operators perform nearly all banking. Discord staff roles create
   unaudited parallel access. Tag management rules are strong. Missing: auditor
   role, scoped access, service-layer defense-in-depth.

2. Money Movement Controls — Deposit/withdraw require approval; instant flows
   (transfer, Alta Pay) do not. Reason required in UI for most actions; server
   gaps on queue review. Silent notification well-implemented where exposed.
   Bulk approve lacks silent toggle. Manual transfer has no customer notify.

3. Approval Workflows — Single-operator everywhere. No expiry on pending
   banking items. Customer confirmation exists for card acceptance and loan
   agreements (not staff dual control). Interbank broken.

4. Operational Safety — Status guards prevent double-approve. Bulk ops
   sequential without batch atomicity. Alta Pay and adjustment reversal lack
   idempotency. Concurrent balance races are the largest safety hole.

5. Administrative Recovery — Good for adjustments, Alta Pay, Card, account
   reopen, company revoke. Bad for approved deposit/withdrawal and loan
   approval. Adjustment reverse API exists without UI.

6. Audit Integrity — Strong for successful paths. Gaps: failed attempts, Discord
   dispatch failures, non-atomic writes, naming inconsistency
   (BANK_PAYMENT_BLOCKED vs denial codes), bot source attribution on Alta
   Private activation.

7. Internal Notes — Append-only, operator-only, audited. visibility column
   unused. No edit/delete (correct). Card notes attach to USER not card entity.

8. Deal Room Operations — V1 threads solid for messaging. Legacy DealRoom has
   officer assignment but participant cleanup on reassignment is weak. Reopen
   breaks Discord. Lock/sync best-effort, non-blocking. Dual deal-room model
   creates confusion.

9. Administrative UX (workflow, not visual UI) — Confirm+reason pattern is
   good. Bulk ops reduce clicks. Gaps: no queue claim, no adjustment reverse in
   console, interbank dead-end, manual transfer invisible to customer, legacy
   deal room dashboard unbounded.

10. Operational Notifications — Staff audit channel well-built with noise
    filtering. Customer operator DMs comprehensive with silent mode. Missing:
    failure alerts, retry, deal room close DM, staff message DM.

11. Data Integrity — Denormalized balances with TOCTOU risk. Unique constraints
    on reference codes and Discord message IDs. No bank reconciliation job.
    Orphan Discord channels possible. Payroll partial failure state ambiguous.

12. Operational Scalability — V1 hardening supports low thousands. Unbounded
    deal room queries, 5k audit export cap, in-memory staff dedupe, sequential
    Discord cleanup, and 100 uncoordinated operators will strain at target
    scale.
