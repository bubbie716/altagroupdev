Alta Bank & Internal Admin Panel — Pre-Production Audit

Audit type: Read-only architectural review
Scope: Alta Bank + Internal Admin Panel only
Assumption: Platform will scale to one of the largest financial institutions on the server
Date: June 22, 2026


================================================================================
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================

Alta Bank has a coherent foundation: Prisma-backed accounts, a denormalized
balance model, atomic $transaction blocks for most instant flows, operator-gated
admin server functions, and a growing audit trail. Core user journeys (open
account, deposit/withdraw request, intrabank transfer, Alta Pay, business
payroll, lending lifecycle) are real and wired to the database.

However, the system is NOT production-ready for an institution of scale. Several
issues are not cosmetic — they are financial integrity and authorization defects
that would cause unauthorized movement, negative balances, duplicate interest,
incorrect loan servicing, and operator error at volume.

The Internal Admin Panel can run day-to-day banking for a small population, but
cannot efficiently or safely operate at 10,000 users / 100 staff without
pagination, queue management, confirmations on money-moving actions, and
reconciliation tooling.


================================================================================
CRITICAL ISSUES
================================================================================

C1. Company viewers can move money from business accounts
    Location: src/server/bank.service.ts — accessibleAccountWhere() grants
    access to any company membership, including viewer and compliance_contact.

    Problem: Business UI restricts treasury actions to manage roles
    (canManageBusinessTreasury), but core bank APIs (submitInternalTransfer,
    submitWithdrawalRequest, etc.) only check accessibleAccountWhere. A
    read-only company member can debit a business operating account server-side.

    Why it matters: Unauthorized treasury access — a launch blocker for business
    banking.


C2. Balance checks occur outside transactions (TOCTOU overdraft)
    Locations:
    - src/server/bank.service.ts — submitInternalTransfer() (~541–552)
    - src/server/alta-pay.service.ts — Alta Pay payments
    - src/server/loan.service.ts — processLoanPayment() (~435–465)

    Problem: getAvailableBalance() is read before the $transaction that
    decrements balance. Concurrent transfers, loan payments, scheduled
    transfers, and payroll can all pass the check and overdraw. There is no DB
    constraint preventing negative BankAccount.balance.

    Why it matters: At any meaningful concurrency, balances become untrustworthy.
    There is no ledger reconciliation to recover.


C3. Withdrawal approval ignores aggregate pending holds
    Location: src/server/bank.service.ts — approveWithdrawal() (~869–871)

    Problem: Submit validates against balance - pending withdrawals. Approval
    only checks raw balance >= amount. Multiple pending withdrawals can all be
    approved until balance goes negative.

    Why it matters: Operators can unknowingly approve more withdrawals than the
    account can support.


C4. availableBalance shown to users is wrong
    Location: src/server/bank.service.ts — getUserBankAccountDetail() (~252)

    Problem: Returns availableBalance: balance (full balance), ignoring pending
    withdrawal holds that getAvailableBalance() correctly subtracts at submit
    time.

    Why it matters: Users see spendable funds they cannot actually withdraw or
    transfer. Creates support load and trust issues.


C5. Loan schedule marked PAID on partial payment
    Location: src/server/loan.service.ts — processLoanPayment() (~494–499)

    Problem: Any payment linked to a schedule item marks that installment PAID,
    even when amount < scheduledAmount (auto-pay uses Math.min(scheduledAmount,
    payoff)). A partial payoff installment is closed; future installments may be
    skipped or misclassified.

    Why it matters: Loan servicing state becomes incorrect. Delinquency tracking
    is explicitly "Coming Soon" — there is no recovery path.


C6. Interest guarantee can double-accrue under concurrency
    Location: src/lib/bank/loan-interest-service.ts —
    guaranteeDueInterestForLoan() (~317–359)

    Problem: Pending schedule items are loaded outside the transaction, then
    updated inside. Concurrent cron runs or operator "Guarantee due interest" +
    cron can both guarantee the same installment.

    Why it matters: Borrowers owe interest that was guaranteed twice. No bank
    debit occurs, but loan payoff inflates — a silent balance corruption.


C7. Duplicate loan servicing cron endpoints
    Locations:
    - src/routes/api/cron/scheduled-transfers.ts — runs loan interest + auto-pay
      in parallel with transfers/payroll
    - src/routes/api/cron/loan-interest.ts — same loan servicing

    Problem: Two cron entry points invoke the same loan batch logic. Auto-pay is
    not safely concurrent across duplicate invocations.

    Why it matters: Double debits on autopay are possible under overlapping
    cron triggers.


C8. Payroll partial execution without compensation
    Location: src/server/payroll-executor.service.ts

    Problem: If line 2 of a payroll run fails after line 1 succeeds, the run is
    marked FAILED but some employees were paid. No saga, rollback, or explicit
    partial-success state.

    Why it matters: Companies pay some employees twice on retry, or operators
    must manually reconcile who was paid.


C9. VITE_UI_LAB_MODE bypasses all authentication
    Locations: src/lib/auth/ui-lab.ts, src/server/auth.service.ts,
    src/lib/auth/guards.ts

    Problem: When enabled, injects a mock admin user and skips auth guards on
    both client and server.

    Why it matters: If this env var is set in any production-like deployment,
    everyone is admin. This is a catastrophic misconfiguration risk.


C10. Public deal room routes are unauthenticated
     Locations:
     - src/routes/bank/lending/deal-rooms/index.tsx
     - src/routes/bank/lending/deal-rooms/$dealRoomId.tsx

     Problem: No authBeforeLoad. Loads mock deal room data for any visitor. UX
     promises secure deal rooms; there is no server enforcement.

     Why it matters: Today exposes mock financial terms to unauthenticated
     users. When wired to real data without auth, this becomes a direct IDOR.


C11. Financial audit writes are not transactional with money movement
     Location: src/server/audit.service.ts + all money-moving services

     Problem: writeAuditLog() runs after the Prisma $transaction commits. Money
     can succeed while audit fails, leaving no operator trail.

     Why it matters: Regulatory and operational requirement for a bank — "who
     moved what money" must be atomic with the movement.


C12. Most money-moving actions have no confirmation
     Location: src/components/bank/bank-review-button.tsx — used across
     deposits, withdrawals, loans, account status, company verify, batch
     operations

     Problem: Single click immediately executes approve/deny/disburse/freeze/
     batch payroll/interest guarantee with no dialog, amount recap, or required
     review note.

     Why it matters: At 100 staff processing queues, misclicks become real
     financial events. private_client revoke (triggers liquidation) also has no
     confirmation.


================================================================================
HIGH PRIORITY
================================================================================

H1. Deposit account interest has no automated cron
     Location: src/lib/bank/account-interest-service.ts —
     accrueInterestForDueAccounts() is admin-triggered only via
     accrueAllDueAccountInterest.

     Impact: Savings/money market/private accounts may never receive scheduled
     interest unless an admin manually runs batch accrual.


H2. Audit logging gaps on high-volume operations

     Logged well: deposit/withdrawal approve/deny, account status changes, admin
     adjustments, manual interest, account interest accrual, loan approve/deny.

     Not logged:
     - User deposit/withdrawal submission — no trail until operator acts
     - Internal transfers — high volume, no actor audit
     - Alta Pay payments — merchant settlement
     - Loan payments & disbursements — core lending gap
     - Scheduled transfer / payroll execution — automated movement
     - Loan freeze/unfreeze/adjust/mark paid off — operator lifecycle
     - Account opening (user-initiated) — only logged on operator approve
     - Private banking liquidation — tag revoke side effect


H3. Operator (not Admin) can disburse loans and adjust balances
     Location: src/lib/bank/lending.functions.ts — all internal records use
     requireOperator(), not requireAdmin().

     Impact: Disbursement, balance adjustment, interest backfill, and auto-pay
     execution are available to any operator. Manual interest apply correctly
     requires admin; loan disbursement does not — inconsistent privilege model.


H4. Disbursement does not re-validate linked account ownership
     Location: src/server/loan.service.ts — approveLoanApplication()

     Problem: linkedBankAccountId validated at application time only. At
     approval, only existence + not frozen/closed is checked. Membership or
     ownership could have changed.


H5. Approval principal uncapped vs requested amount
     Location: src/server/loan.service.ts + internal-lending-panel.tsx

     Problem: Operator can set principalAmount to any positive value at approval
     with no ceiling vs requestedAmount.


H6. Interbank scheduled transfers never execute
     Location: src/server/scheduled-transfer.service.ts

     Problem: Interbank schedules sit in PENDING_REVIEW with no wire execution
     path. Users can create them; nothing happens.


H7. Companies admin pages fall back to mock data on DB error
     Location: src/routes/internal/companies/index.tsx, $companyId.tsx

     Problem: Catch blocks show fake company data. Operators may act on records
     that do not exist.


H8. Admin list endpoints silently truncate

     Users: 200
     Audit log: 200
     Bank accounts: 500
     Pending deposits/withdrawals: Unbounded (loads all)
     Loan applications: Unbounded
     Scheduled transfers: Unbounded

     Impact: Staff cannot see the full population. No pagination, no "load
     more", no export.


H9. No idempotency on user-facing instant payments

     Problem: Internal transfers, Alta Pay, loan payments have no client
     idempotency key. Network retries can double-pay (reference code collision
     would fail, but a retry generates a new reference).

     Contrast: Manual interest apply has idempotency via audit log — the right
     pattern, applied inconsistently.


H10. Cron secret accepted via query parameter
      Location: src/routes/api/cron/scheduled-transfers.ts (~22–23)

      Problem: ?secret= may leak via logs, referrers, browser history.


H11. markLoanPaidOff / adminAdjustLoanBalance leave inconsistent state

      Problem:
      - markLoanPaidOff waives interest but does not mark principal schedule
        rows PAID
      - adminAdjustLoanBalance can zero payoff while loan stays ACTIVE


H12. Service-layer permission checks inconsistent

      Problem: Some services call requireOperator() internally
      (adminAdjustBankAccount, getInternalBankAccountDetail); others
      (approveDeposit, listInternalBankAccounts) rely entirely on server
      function boundary. Any future import bypasses auth.


================================================================================
MEDIUM PRIORITY
================================================================================

M1. Scheduled transfer executor impersonates creator
     Location: src/server/scheduled-transfer-executor.service.ts
     Runs transfers as createdByUserId. If membership is revoked, executor
     behavior is undefined relative to current treasury policy.

M2. No transfer pair linkage in schema
     Location: prisma/schema.prisma — BankTransaction
     Intrabank transfers create paired WITHDRAWAL/DEPOSIT rows linked only by
     reference naming (TRF-*-OUT/*-IN). No FK or journal grouping.

M3. Statement void exists in schema but no admin UI
     Location: BankStatementStatus.VOID in schema; statement.service.ts counts
     voided statements; no void mutation exposed in admin.

M4. Compliance page is a placeholder
     Location: src/routes/internal/compliance.tsx
     Counters only. No case model, assignment, or investigation workflow.

M5. Company admin is verify-only
     No suspend/reactivate, ticker assignment, internal notes, linked bank
     accounts, or audit on company detail page.

M6. User detail page bank accounts are read-only
     No deep link to /internal/bank/accounts/$accountId from user profile.

M7. Side effects on read
     Location: src/server/loan.service.ts — getUserLoans() calls
     ensureLoanPaymentSchedule() during list fetch, mutating DB on page load.

M8. Deal rooms are 100% mock with no loan integration
     Locations: src/lib/bank/deal-rooms-mock.ts, internal/lending.deal-rooms.tsx,
     public deal room routes
     Application UX references deal rooms opening; nothing is created server-side.

M9. Internal settings feature flags are static placeholders
     Location: src/routes/internal/settings.tsx, src/lib/internal/data.ts
     Not persisted. No maintenance mode, no remote toggles.

M10. Markets nav items are mock but presented as operational
      Exchange, IPOs, listings, terminal, API applications — all mock data with
      disabled buttons. Only exchange has a preview notice; others do not.

M11. No reconciliation tooling
      No balance vs transaction sum verification, no end-of-day reports, no
      exception queue for negative balances or orphaned transactions.

M12. Float amounts throughout
      Many inputs accept JavaScript floats without strict decimal validation.

M13. Audit log action is free string, not enum
      Location: prisma/schema.prisma — AuditLog.action
      Flexible but unenforceable. Inconsistent action naming over time.

M14. Deposit approval does not re-check account status
      Frozen account could receive approved deposit if request was submitted
      while active.

================================================================================
RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

Immediate (pre-launch blockers):

1. Align server-side account access with treasury RBAC — require
   canManageBusinessTreasury for all mutating ops on business accounts.
2. Move balance checks inside $transaction with conditional update
   (WHERE balance >= amount) or row locking.
3. Fix withdrawal approval to respect aggregate pending holds.
4. Fix availableBalance in account detail to match submit-time logic.
5. Fix loan schedule PAID logic — only mark PAID when cumulative payment >=
   scheduled amount; support partial state.
6. Make interest guarantee idempotent — conditional status transition
   PENDING -> GUARANTEED WHERE status = PENDING.
7. Consolidate loan cron to one endpoint or add per-loan/per-account lock.
8. Add confirmation dialogs to all money-moving admin actions with amount recap
   and optional review note.
9. Guard VITE_UI_LAB_MODE — build-time fail if set in production.
10. Add authBeforeLoad to deal room routes before any real data wiring.

Short-term (first month of operations):

11. Add cron for deposit account interest accrual.
12. Audit all money-moving mutations inside the same transaction as the
    financial write.
13. Pagination + server-side search on users, accounts, audit, all queues.
14. Remove mock fallbacks on company routes — fail loudly.
15. Split operator vs admin for loan disbursement, large adjustments, batch ops.
16. Client idempotency keys on instant payment endpoints.
17. Payroll saga — all lines in one transaction or explicit partial-success.
18. Re-validate disbursement account ownership at loan approval.

Medium-term (institution scale):

19. Balance reconciliation job (sum of transactions vs BankAccount.balance).
20. Queue model with assignee, SLA, bulk approve/deny, optimistic locking.
21. Compliance case management workflow.
22. Reporting/export for audit and regulatory investigation.
23. Transfer pair journal grouping in schema.
24. DB check constraints on non-negative balances.
25. Wire interbank execution or remove interbank schedule creation from user UI.

2. LOAN SYSTEM

Solid permission scaffolding (canUserPayLoan, userLoanViewWhere), good DB
uniqueness on schedule items and payment links. Undermined by schedule PAID bug,
guarantee race, operator-level disbursement, missing audit on
payments/disbursements, and mock deal rooms.


3. ADMIN PANEL — Can Staff Run the Bank?

Yes, for a small population (< ~500 accounts, few concurrent operators).

No, at stated scale without:
- Pagination and search across all queues
- Bulk operations
- Queue assignment
- Confirmations on financial actions
- Reconciliation and exception handling
- Automated interest accrual
- Compliance workflow

Still requires DB edits for:
- Statement void
- Company suspension beyond verify/reject
- Interbank transfer execution
- Compliance cases
- Feature flags / maintenance mode
- Deal room lifecycle
- Reversing errant batch operations without manual adjustment per account


4. SECURITY

Works: Route guard on /internal, server-side requireOperator/requireAdmin on
live mutations, frozen/restricted staff blocked, payment amount validated
server-side.

Fails: Treasury RBAC mismatch (C1), TOCTOU (C2), UI Lab bypass (C9),
unauthenticated deal rooms (C10), cron secret in query string (H10), operator
disbursement privileges (H3).


5. DATA CONSISTENCY

------------------  -----------  -----------  --------------  -----
Deposit approve     Yes          Yes          Status guard    Yes
Withdrawal approve  Yes          Hold bug     Status guard    Yes
Internal transfer   Yes          Race         Ref unique only No
Alta Pay            Yes          Race         Ref unique only No
Account interest    Yes          Yes          Period unique   Yes
Manual interest     Per-acct tx  Yes          Idempotency key Yes
Admin adjustment    Yes          Yes          No              Yes
Loan disbursement   Yes          Yes          App unique      No
Loan payment        Yes          Race         Schedule unique No

6. OPERATIONAL WORKFLOW AT SCALE

Bottlenecks: Single-click queues, no assignee, no bulk approve, mega-loader on
bank hub, keystroke-triggered user search, unbounded pending queues, global
batch buttons, no export.

Missing: Work queues with SLA, staff workload dashboard, exception/alerts for
negative balances, end-of-day settlement report, investigation tools beyond
200-row audit cap.


7. AUDIT LOGGING

Strong for operator review actions and manual interest. Weak for user-initiated
and automated money movement. Audit not co-transactional with financial writes.
targetLoanId field exists but rarely populated.


8. UX (WORKFLOW EFFICIENCY)

Good: Manual interest multi-step preview + confirmation phrase; account
adjustment has confirm dialog; admin tag changes have dialogs.

Poor: BankReviewButton pattern everywhere else; bank hub overloads one page;
user -> account navigation broken; interest page not in sub-nav; companies have
no search; duplicate transfers/scheduled views; lending batch buttons on same
page as application queue.


9. DATABASE DESIGN

Strengths: Good indexes on status/dates; unique constraints on interest accrual
periods, schedule installments, payment schedule links, execution idempotency
keys.

Gaps: No non-negative balance CHECK; no transfer pair FK; no DB constraint
linking loan disbursement account to applicant; denormalized loan balances must
stay in sync via application code; AuditLog.action is unenforced string;
PayrollRun.lineItems as JSON.


10. PERFORMANCE

N+1 risk in list mappers; bank hub loader fetches everything upfront;
AdminDataTable renders full DOM (no virtualization); unbounded loan/scheduled
transfer lists; no caching on dashboard metrics; concurrent cron debiting same
accounts in parallel (Promise.all in scheduled-transfers cron).

At 10k users: user list cap hides 98% of users; account search cap hides
records; audit unusable for investigation.

================================================================================
VERDICT
================================================================================

Alta Bank is a functional beta suitable for controlled testing with a small user
base and low transaction concurrency. It is NOT ready for production launch as
a major server financial institution until the Critical and Launch Blocker items
are resolved. The admin panel provides real operational capability but lacks the
safety rails, scale infrastructure, and audit completeness required before real
money (even simulated) moves at institution volume.
