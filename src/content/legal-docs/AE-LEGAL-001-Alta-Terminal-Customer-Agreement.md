## 1. Overview

This Alta Terminal Customer Agreement (“Agreement”) governs access to and use of Alta Terminal.

Alta Terminal is a brokerage-style trading, portfolio, and market access platform operated by **Alta Exchange**, a wholly owned subsidiary of Alta Group N.V.

By opening an Alta Terminal account, placing orders, viewing holdings, trading securities, using market tools, or accessing related services, the customer agrees to this Agreement, the Alta Group Terms of Service, the Alta Group Privacy Policy, the Alta Exchange Trading Rules, any applicable fee schedule, and any other rules or disclosures provided by Alta Exchange.

Alta Exchange may update this Agreement at any time.

---

## 2. Roleplay and Virtual Economy Notice

Alta Terminal is designed for use within Minecraft, Discord, roleplay communities, virtual economies, and related online environments.

Unless Alta Exchange clearly states otherwise in writing:

- Alta Terminal is not a real-world brokerage;
- Alta Exchange is not a real-world securities exchange;
- Alta Terminal accounts are not real-world brokerage accounts;
- securities, shares, balances, orders, trades, positions, dividends, and market data may represent in-game, fictional, simulated, or roleplay value only;
- use of Alta Terminal does not create real-world investment, securities, banking, legal, or financial rights;
- Alta Terminal does not provide real-world investment advice.

Customers are responsible for understanding that Alta Terminal is a roleplay and virtual economy product unless expressly stated otherwise.

## 5. Customer Account

An Alta Terminal account allows a customer to view market information, submit orders, hold positions, track portfolios, and access trading tools approved by Alta Exchange.

A customer account does not create ownership in Alta Exchange, Alta Group, Alta Terminal, or any Alta subsidiary.

Customers may not sell, transfer, assign, share, or give control of an Alta Terminal account to another person without Alta Exchange approval.

Alta Exchange may rely on its records to determine account ownership, permissions, positions, balances, and trading history.

The customer is responsible for protecting access to their Alta Terminal account, Discord account, Minecraft account, email, device, and login credentials.

The customer agrees not to:

- allow unauthorized users to access the account;
- use another person’s account;
- impersonate another person or entity;
- bypass security controls;
- exploit bugs or technical errors;
- attempt unauthorized access to Alta systems;
- submit false information.

## 7. Trading Authority

By submitting an order through Alta Terminal, the customer authorizes Alta Exchange to process the order according to the customer’s instructions, this Agreement, the Alta Exchange Trading Rules, and applicable platform procedures.

The customer is responsible for verifying:

- the security;
- ticker symbol;
- order type;
- quantity;
- price;
- side of trade;
- account;
- timing;
- estimated fees;
- any other order details.

Alta Exchange may rely on orders submitted through an authenticated account.

## 8. Orders

Alta Terminal may support order types approved by Alta Exchange.

These may include:

- market orders;
- limit orders;
- stop orders;
- cancellation requests;
- modification requests;
- other order types approved by Alta Exchange.

## 9. Order Acceptance, Rejection, and Cancellation

Alta Exchange may accept, reject, cancel, modify, delay, route, hold, or review an order when necessary.

Reasons may include:

- insufficient funds;
- insufficient securities;
- account restrictions;
- trading halts;
- market closure;
- invalid order details;
- suspicious activity;
- market manipulation concerns;
- technical issues;
- system delays;
- listing restrictions;
- risk controls;
- violation of trading rules;
- platform integrity concerns.

Alta Exchange is not required to accept or execute every order.

## 10. Execution and Trade Finality

A trade is not final until completed and recorded by Alta Exchange.

Alta Exchange may cancel, correct, adjust, or reverse trades when necessary to address:

- obvious errors;
- duplicate trades;
- technical errors;
- unauthorized activity;
- market manipulation;
- invalid orders;
- settlement failure;
- system issues;
- rule violations;
- platform integrity concerns.

Alta Exchange records are the official records of orders, executions, trades, positions, and account activity unless Alta Exchange determines that a correction is required.

## 11. Settlement

Alta Exchange may establish settlement procedures for trades completed through Alta Terminal.

Settlement may involve transfer of securities, balances, credits, debits, fees, or other records within Alta systems.

Alta Exchange may delay, fail, reverse, or adjust settlement if necessary to resolve errors, insufficient funds, insufficient securities, account restrictions, disputes, technical issues, rule violations, or platform integrity concerns.

## 12. Securities and Holdings

Alta Terminal may display securities, shares, units, interests, or other market instruments approved by Alta Exchange.

Holdings displayed in Alta Terminal represent the customer’s recorded position within the Alta Exchange system.

A displayed holding may be restricted, pending, unsettled, suspended, frozen, or otherwise limited depending on the status of the security, account, order, or market.

Alta Exchange may correct holdings if it determines that an error, duplicate record, invalid trade, reversal, split, dividend, corporate action, or other adjustment occurred.

## 13. Cash Balances and Buying Power

Alta Terminal may display cash balances, buying power, available funds, pending funds, unsettled funds, restricted funds, or other account values.

Alta Exchange may hold, restrict, adjust, or correct balances when necessary to address settlement, pending trades, fees, disputes, fraud, technical issues, account restrictions, or other operational concerns.
