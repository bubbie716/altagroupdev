# NCC-COR-001 — NCC Governance Charter

**Entity:** Newport Clearing Corporation (“NCC”)  
**Document ID:** NCC-COR-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active upon approval  
**Approved By:** Alta Group N.V.  
**Effective Date:** Immediately upon approval  
**Last Updated:** July 6, 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

Newport Clearing Corporation (“NCC”) is a wholly owned subsidiary of Alta Group N.V. responsible for operating clearing, settlement, routing, and interbank infrastructure within the Alta ecosystem.

NCC exists to provide neutral financial infrastructure for approved institutions, including:

- interbank transfers;
- settlement between financial institutions;
- payment routing;
- routing number administration;
- clearing records;
- institution participation records;
- settlement reporting;
- infrastructure rules; and
- related financial network services approved by Alta Group.

NCC is intended to operate as infrastructure, not as a retail bank.

---

## 2. Ownership

NCC is owned and controlled by Alta Group N.V.

No person, institution, bank, participant, customer, or outside entity shall obtain ownership, voting power, management authority, or control over NCC unless approved by Alta Group.

Participation in NCC does not create ownership in NCC or Alta Group.

Alta Group may reorganize, merge, rename, expand, restrict, suspend, or dissolve NCC at its discretion.

---

## 3. Relationship with Alta Group

NCC operates under the authority of Alta Group.

Alta Group retains final authority over:

- appointment or removal of NCC leadership;
- approval of participating institutions;
- major operating rules;
- settlement rules;
- routing standards;
- fees and revenue models;
- technology systems;
- risk controls;
- major expenses;
- capital allocation;
- debt or financing;
- legal documents;
- branding;
- mergers, reorganizations, or dissolution; and
- any matter reserved to Alta Group under its corporate documents.

NCC may conduct normal daily operations without separate approval unless the matter is reserved to Alta Group.

---

## 4. Management

NCC shall be managed by a Director appointed by the Chairman of Alta Group.

The Director is responsible for daily operations, including clearing administration, settlement operations, institution onboarding, routing records, staff supervision, and implementation of Alta Group directives.

The Chairman of Alta Group may remove or replace the Director at any time.

The Director may appoint managers, employees, contractors, operators, or agents as needed to support NCC operations, subject to Alta Group authority.

---

## 5. Authority of NCC

Subject to Alta Group oversight, NCC may:

- operate clearing and settlement systems;
- maintain routing number records;
- approve, deny, suspend, or remove participating institutions;
- process interbank transfer records;
- establish settlement procedures;
- maintain institution profiles;
- publish operating rules;
- charge participation, routing, settlement, or network fees;
- investigate settlement issues;
- pause or reject unsafe transactions;
- create internal operating procedures;
- communicate with participating institutions; and
- take actions reasonably necessary to operate financial infrastructure.

---

## 6. Limitations

NCC may not do any of the following without Alta Group approval:

- issue equity or ownership interests;
- create a subsidiary;
- borrow money;
- approve major expenses outside approved budgets;
- sell or transfer major assets;
- transfer intellectual property;
- materially change its business model;
- enter into major partnerships;
- waive important legal rights;
- change its name or branding;
- amend this Charter;
- dissolve, merge, or reorganize;
- admit a participant outside approved standards; or
- take any action reserved to Alta Group.

---

## 7. Participating Institutions

Approved banks, financial institutions, platforms, or other entities may participate in NCC subject to NCC rules, participation agreements, technical requirements, and approval standards.

NCC may require participating institutions to:

- maintain accurate institution information;
- follow settlement rules;
- maintain sufficient balances or settlement capacity;
- comply with routing procedures;
- respond to disputes or investigations;
- maintain accurate transaction records;
- pay applicable fees;
- cooperate with audits or reviews; and
- follow NCC operating standards.

NCC may suspend, restrict, or remove a participating institution when necessary to protect the network, prevent abuse, resolve settlement issues, or enforce NCC rules.

---

## 8. Clearing and Settlement

NCC may establish procedures for the clearing and settlement of transfers between participating institutions.

These procedures may address:

- transfer submission;
- routing validation;
- settlement timing;
- pending transfers;
- failed transfers;
- reversals;
- disputes;
- settlement balances;
- institution obligations;
- operating hours;
- reporting;
- fees; and
- emergency restrictions.

NCC may delay, reject, reverse, or investigate transactions when necessary to protect the integrity of the network.

---

## 9. Routing Numbers and Institution Records

NCC may assign, maintain, suspend, revoke, or update routing numbers and institution identifiers for approved participants.

A routing number or institution identifier does not create ownership in NCC and may be revoked or modified under NCC rules.

Participating institutions are responsible for keeping their records accurate and current.

---

## 10. Risk and Network Integrity

NCC may adopt controls designed to protect network integrity.

These controls may include:

- transaction limits;
- participant reviews;
- settlement holds;
- suspicious activity flags;
- manual review;
- emergency pauses;
- institution restrictions;
- audit logs;
- reversal procedures; and
- dispute procedures.

NCC may take immediate action when needed to protect the clearing network, Alta Group, participating institutions, or users.

---

## 11. Financial Reporting

NCC shall maintain records of its revenue, expenses, assets, liabilities, fees, settlement activity, and other financial activity.

NCC shall provide financial information to Alta Group upon request.

Alta Group may consolidate NCC financial activity into Alta Group financial statements.

---

## 12. Technology and Intellectual Property

All software, websites, APIs, bots, code, databases, routing systems, settlement systems, trademarks, trade names, domains, designs, documents, and other intellectual property used by NCC are owned by Alta Group unless Alta Group states otherwise in writing.

NCC receives an internal license to use Alta Group technology and intellectual property for its approved business operations.

NCC may not sell, transfer, license, copy, or assign Alta Group intellectual property without Alta Group approval.

---

## 13. Branding

NCC may use the NCC name, logos, bridge marks, designs, and related branding only with permission from Alta Group.

Alta Group may update, restrict, or revoke branding rules at any time.

---

## 14. Compliance with Alta Group Documents

NCC shall operate consistently with:

- Alta Group Articles of Incorporation;
- Alta Group Bylaws;
- Alta Group Shareholders' Agreement;
- Alta Group Reserved Powers Resolution;
- this Charter;
- NCC participation agreements;
- NCC settlement rules;
- NCC operating rules; and
- any other applicable Alta Group or NCC documents.

If there is a conflict, Alta Group documents control unless Alta Group decides otherwise.

---

## 15. Amendments

Alta Group may amend, replace, suspend, or terminate this Charter at any time.

No amendment shall be valid unless approved by Alta Group.

---

## 16. Approval

This Charter is adopted by Alta Group as the governing document for NCC.
