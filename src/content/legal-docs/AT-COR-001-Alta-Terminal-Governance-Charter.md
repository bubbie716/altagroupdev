# AT-COR-001 — Alta Terminal Governance Charter

**Entity:** Alta Terminal LLC  
**Document ID:** AT-COR-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Active upon approval  
**Approved By:** Alta Group N.V.  
**Effective Date:** Immediately upon approval  
**Last Updated:** July 21, 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

Alta Terminal LLC (“Alta Terminal”) is a subsidiary of Alta Group N.V. responsible for brokerage-style account, portfolio, research, order-entry, and market-access services within supported virtual economies.

Alta Terminal exists to connect eligible Customers to approved market infrastructure while providing a consistent Alta account experience. Alta Terminal is not itself a trading venue, securities exchange, bank, clearing agency, or real-world regulated broker-dealer.

## 2. Ownership

Alta Terminal is wholly owned and controlled by Alta Group N.V. unless Alta Group approves a different ownership arrangement in writing.

Customers, issuers, vendors, data providers, execution providers, employees, contractors, and counterparties do not acquire ownership or governance rights through their use of or relationship with Alta Terminal.

## 3. Relationship with Alta Group

Alta Group retains final authority over:

- ownership and capitalization;
- appointment and removal of Terminal leadership;
- material strategy and product scope;
- customer-facing agreements and disclosures;
- selection of material execution, custody, data, banking, and settlement providers;
- fees and material commercial arrangements;
- risk appetite and control standards;
- major technology changes;
- branding and intellectual property;
- mergers, reorganization, suspension, sale, or dissolution; and
- matters reserved under Alta Group corporate documents.

Alta Terminal may conduct ordinary operations within approved policies and budgets.

## 4. Management

Alta Terminal shall be managed by a Director or President appointed by Alta Group. The appointed leader is responsible for daily operations, product delivery, customer support, provider coordination, staff supervision, risk escalation, and implementation of Alta Group directives.

The appointed leader may assign operational roles subject to Alta Group oversight. No operational title limits Alta Group’s reserved authority.

## 5. Authorized Activities

Subject to feature readiness, provider availability, and Alta Group approval, Alta Terminal may:

- open, maintain, restrict, and close Customer accounts;
- provide portfolio, watchlist, research, statement, and reporting tools;
- receive and validate Customer order instructions;
- route eligible instructions to approved independent providers;
- reconcile provider confirmations, positions, balances, and corporate actions;
- provide market and reference data obtained from approved sources;
- collect disclosed fees;
- investigate errors, abuse, fraud, or security events;
- maintain records and audit trails;
- publish Customer agreements, disclosures, and operational procedures; and
- perform other brokerage-support activities approved by Alta Group.

## 6. Prohibited or Reserved Activities

Alta Terminal may not, without an express amendment approved by Alta Group:

- operate or represent itself as a securities exchange or trading venue;
- represent itself as a real-world regulated broker, adviser, bank, custodian, or clearing agency;
- offer margin, lending, short selling, options, discretionary management, or securities lending;
- guarantee returns, liquidity, execution, settlement, or provider performance;
- issue ownership interests in Alta Terminal;
- create a subsidiary;
- borrow funds or pledge material assets;
- enter a material provider or partnership agreement;
- transfer material intellectual property;
- materially change its business model; or
- dissolve, merge, sell, or reorganize.

## 7. Provider Model

Alta Terminal may depend on independent providers for execution, venue access, custody or position records, settlement, market data, identity, communications, or banking services.

Before enabling a provider-dependent feature, Alta Terminal shall document:

- the provider’s responsibilities;
- Alta Terminal’s responsibilities;
- authentication and authorization controls;
- error, cancellation, and reconciliation procedures;
- service limits and failure handling;
- data use and retention;
- financial and operational dependencies; and
- termination and transition procedures.

Provider appointment does not transfer Alta Terminal’s responsibility for accurate Customer disclosures or reasonable oversight of its own systems.

## 8. Customer Protection Principles

Alta Terminal shall seek to:

- distinguish live features from planned or simulated features;
- disclose material fees and risks before Customer action;
- use authenticated, idempotent instructions where appropriate;
- maintain understandable account and transaction records;
- separate Customer permissions from staff permissions;
- protect credentials and sensitive information;
- investigate reported errors consistently;
- identify material affiliate or provider conflicts; and
- avoid representing fictional services as real-world regulated products.

## 9. Order and Account Controls

Alta Terminal may establish eligibility, funding, instrument, quantity, price, velocity, concentration, and account controls. Controls shall be documented, consistently administered, and capable of preventing or restricting unsafe activity.

Orders may be rejected or held before transmission when they fail validation or risk controls. Provider responses shall be recorded and reconciled to Customer-facing status.

## 10. Records and Audit

Alta Terminal shall maintain records reasonably necessary to explain:

- account approval and permissions;
- Customer instructions;
- provider transmissions and responses;
- executions, cancellations, corrections, and failures;
- positions and cash movements;
- fees;
- staff actions;
- disputes and resolutions; and
- material operational incidents.

Production records shall not be silently replaced by mock, preview, or test data.

## 11. Conflicts and Affiliates

Alta Terminal may use services provided by Alta Group affiliates, including Alta Bank. Affiliate use must not be presented as independent advice or independent provider selection.

Material compensation, preferential routing, ownership, or provider relationships that could affect Customer activity shall be disclosed when relevant.

## 12. Risk and Incident Management

Alta Terminal shall maintain procedures for provider outages, stale or incorrect data, duplicate instructions, reconciliation breaks, unauthorized access, compromised credentials, market interruptions, and service suspension.

Material incidents shall be escalated to Alta Group and documented through appropriate operational and audit channels.

## 13. Legal and Customer Documents

Alta Terminal shall maintain, at minimum:

- a Customer Agreement;
- Trading and Order Handling Terms;
- a Risk Disclosure;
- Market Data and Third-Party Services Terms; and
- a Fee Schedule.

Additional agreements shall be adopted before margin, lending, APIs, advisory services, institutional accounts, or other materially different products are enabled.

## 14. Amendments

Alta Group may amend this Charter. Alta Terminal management may propose amendments but may not adopt them without Alta Group approval.

## 15. Approval

This Charter establishes Alta Terminal as a standalone Alta Group subsidiary and supersedes any prior description of Terminal as a product or division of another market operator.
