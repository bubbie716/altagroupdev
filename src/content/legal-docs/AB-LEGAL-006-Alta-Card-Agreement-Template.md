# **ARTICLE 3: ISSUANCE, CREDIT LIMIT, AND ACCOUNT USE**

## **3.1 Card Issuance**

Upon approval and execution of this Agreement, the Issuer may establish an Alta Card Account for the Cardholder and make credit available up to the Credit Limit, subject to this Agreement and any restrictions imposed by the Issuer.

The Issuer is not required to issue a physical, digital, visual, or separate card instrument unless the Issuer chooses to do so. The Alta Card may exist solely as a credit account within Alta Bank systems.

## **3.2 Credit Limit**

The Cardholder may not intentionally exceed the Credit Limit.

The Issuer may approve or reject any Charge that would exceed the Credit Limit. If the Issuer allows a Charge that exceeds the Credit Limit, the Cardholder remains responsible for the full Outstanding Balance, including the over-limit amount.

## **3.3 Changes to Credit Limit**

The Issuer may increase, decrease, freeze, suspend, or cancel the Credit Limit at any time based on account history, payment performance, risk, collateral value, platform conditions, or the Issuer's business judgment.

Unless required by a specific written term, the Issuer is not obligated to provide advance notice before reducing, suspending, or freezing the Credit Limit.

## **3.4 Use of Card**

The Cardholder may use the Alta Card only for approved transactions and only within the limits of this Agreement, the Card Terms Schedule, Alta Bank rules, Alta Group rules, and applicable platform or server rules.

## **3.5 Use Restrictions**

The Cardholder shall comply with all Use Restrictions specified in the Card Terms Schedule or otherwise communicated by the Issuer.

The Issuer may waive, modify, or add Use Restrictions by written notice to the Cardholder.

# **ARTICLE 4: CHARGES, STATEMENTS, AND RECORDS**

## **4.1 Charges**

The Cardholder is responsible for all Charges posted to the Account, including Charges made by authorized users, representatives, agents, employees, or any person the Cardholder permits to access or use the Account.

## **4.2 Unauthorized Charges**

The Cardholder shall notify the Issuer promptly upon discovering any suspected unauthorized Charge, compromised account access, mistaken transaction, or other account issue.

The Issuer may investigate the issue and may rely on account logs, platform records, Discord records, screenshots, staff review, transaction data, and other available evidence.

## **4.3 Statements**

The Issuer may provide Statements through the Alta Bank website, dashboard, Discord, email, deal room, direct message, or other approved communication method.

The Cardholder is responsible for reviewing each Statement and reporting errors promptly.

## **4.4 Records Control**

The Issuer's records are the official records of the Account unless the Issuer determines that a correction is required.

The Issuer may correct, adjust, reverse, cancel, or reclassify Charges, payments, fees, interest, limits, Collateral records, or account history if necessary to address errors, duplication, fraud, platform issues, technical issues, or other account concerns.

# **ARTICLE 5: INTEREST AND FEES**

## **5.1 Interest Accrual**

Interest shall accrue on the Outstanding Balance at the Interest Rate specified in the Card Terms Schedule, calculated and applied according to the Interest Type and Compounding Period specified.

Interest shall begin accruing on the applicable date determined by the Card Terms Schedule or by the Issuer's standard card rules.

## **5.2 Grace Period**

If a Grace Period is provided, eligible Charges may avoid interest only if the Cardholder satisfies the requirements of the Grace Period.

Unless the Card Terms Schedule states otherwise, no Grace Period applies after a missed payment, Default, account restriction, or carried balance.

## **5.3 Fees**

The Cardholder shall pay all fees specified in the Card Terms Schedule, any applicable Alta Bank Fee Schedule, or any written notice provided by the Issuer.

Fees may include annual fees, monthly fees, late fees, over-limit fees, cash advance fees, transfer fees, replacement fees, administrative fees, collection costs, enforcement costs, or other disclosed fees.

## **5.4 Late Payment Interest**

If the Cardholder fails to make a required payment when due, Late Payment Interest shall accrue on the overdue payment amount at the rate specified in the Card Terms Schedule.

Late Payment Interest is in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other remedies available to the Issuer.

## **5.5 Default Interest Rate**

Upon the occurrence and continuation of an Event of Default, the Outstanding Balance may bear interest at the Default Interest Rate specified in the Card Terms Schedule until the Event of Default is cured or until all amounts are paid in full.

If no Default Interest Rate is specified, interest shall continue to accrue at the regular Interest Rate.

# **ARTICLE 6: PAYMENTS**

## **6.1 Minimum Payment**

The Cardholder shall pay at least the Minimum Payment by each Due Date.

Payment of the Minimum Payment does not prevent interest from accruing on the remaining Outstanding Balance unless the Card Terms Schedule states otherwise.

## **6.2 Full Payment**

The Cardholder may pay the Outstanding Balance in full at any time.

Payment in full shall not prevent the Issuer from later posting Charges, fees, interest, adjustments, corrections, reversals, or other amounts properly owed under this Agreement.

## **6.3 Application of Payments**

Unless otherwise directed by the Issuer in writing, all payments received by the Issuer shall be applied in the following order of priority:

1. First, to unpaid fees, costs, and expenses;
2. Second, to Late Payment Interest;
3. Third, to accrued and unpaid interest at the Default Interest Rate, if applicable;
4. Fourth, to accrued and unpaid interest at the regular Interest Rate;
5. Fifth, to overdue Charges;
6. Sixth, to current Charges;
7. Seventh, to any remaining Outstanding Balance.

## **6.4 Method of Payment**

All payments shall be made to the Issuer's designated account or through an approved Alta Bank payment method.

Payments made by any other method must be approved in advance by the Issuer.

## **6.5 Currency**

All payments shall be made in the Card Currency specified in the Card Terms Schedule, unless the Issuer approves another currency in writing.

## **6.6 No Offset**

The Cardholder waives any right to offset any amounts owed to the Issuer under this Agreement against any amounts the Cardholder claims the Issuer owes to the Cardholder for any reason.

# **ARTICLE 7: COLLATERAL AND SECURITY INTERESTS**

## **7.1 Secured Card**

If the Alta Card is designated as a Secured Card in the Card Terms Schedule, the Cardholder grants to the Issuer a security interest in all Collateral listed in the Card Terms Schedule.

The security interest secures all obligations under this Agreement, including the Outstanding Balance, interest, fees, costs, expenses, enforcement costs, and any other amounts owed.

## **7.2 Security Deposit or Pledged Assets**

Collateral may include a security deposit, pledged funds, pledged assets, guarantees, account holds, business assets, or other property accepted by the Issuer.

The Issuer may hold, restrict, freeze, or apply Collateral as permitted by this Agreement.

## **7.3 Collateral Value**

The Issuer may determine the value of Collateral in its reasonable discretion.

If the Issuer determines that the Collateral is insufficient, impaired, unavailable, disputed, double-pledged, or reduced in value, the Issuer may require additional Collateral, reduce the Credit Limit, freeze the Account, or declare a Default.

## **7.4 No Double-Pledging**

The Cardholder shall not pledge the same Collateral to another lender, creditor, card issuer, bank, institution, or party without the Issuer's written consent.

## **7.5 Application of Collateral**

Upon Default, closure, cancellation, unpaid balance, or other enforcement event, the Issuer may apply Collateral to amounts owed under this Agreement.

Collateral proceeds shall be applied first to enforcement costs and expenses, then to fees, then to interest, then to Charges and Outstanding Balance. Any surplus shall be handled according to applicable rules, court order, agreement, or platform procedure.

## **7.6 Release of Collateral**

Collateral may be released only after all obligations under this Agreement are fully satisfied and the Issuer determines that no pending Charges, disputes, fees, interest, reversals, or other obligations remain.

# **ARTICLE 10: EVENTS OF DEFAULT**

## **10.1 Events of Default**

Each of the following constitutes an Event of Default:

1. failure to make a required payment when due;
2. failure to pay the Outstanding Balance when required;
3. exceeding the Credit Limit without curing the over-limit amount when requested;
4. unauthorized, fraudulent, abusive, or prohibited use of the Card;
5. false or misleading information provided to the Issuer;
6. failure to provide requested documentation or information;
7. impairment, loss, double-pledging, or reduction of Collateral;
8. failure to maintain required Collateral;
9. account takeover, unauthorized access, or suspected compromise;
10. violation of this Agreement or any Alta Bank or Alta Group agreement;
11. violation of applicable server rules, platform rules, or recognized roleplay law;
12. insolvency, bankruptcy-style filing, or inability to pay debts as they become due;
13. transfer, concealment, or disposal of assets to avoid repayment;
14. abuse of disputes, refunds, reversals, or payment systems;
15. any event that the Issuer reasonably determines creates unacceptable credit, security, fraud, or platform risk.

## **10.2 Notice and Cure**

The Issuer may, but is not required to, provide notice and an opportunity to cure a Default.

If a cure period is provided, the Cardholder must cure the Default within the period stated by the Issuer.

The Issuer may act immediately without notice if necessary to protect the Issuer, Alta Bank, Alta Group, users, merchants, institutions, Collateral, or platform integrity.
