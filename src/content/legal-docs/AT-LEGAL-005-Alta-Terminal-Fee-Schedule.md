## 3. Provider and Pass-Through Charges

An independent execution, data, custody, settlement, payment, or banking provider may impose a charge. Alta Terminal will disclose a known required charge before the Customer confirms the affected instruction whenever reasonably practicable.

Alta Terminal will not silently convert a third-party charge into an undisclosed markup.

## 4. Corrections and Exceptional Work

Alta Terminal does not currently impose routine correction, cancellation, support, inactivity, or account-closing fees.

If a Customer requests exceptional manual work that would create a charge, Alta Terminal must disclose and obtain acceptance of that charge before performing the work, unless immediate action is necessary to contain fraud or a security incident.

## 5. Future Fees

Before an enabled trading, premium data, subscription, institutional, or API service charges a fee, Alta Terminal will publish the amount or calculation method and identify when it applies.

Fee changes apply prospectively. A fee shown on an accepted order or service confirmation controls for that instruction unless corrected before execution with the Customer’s consent.

## 6. Deductions and Insufficient Funds

An authorized fee may be deducted from available virtual cash or transaction proceeds. Alta Terminal may reject an instruction if available funds are insufficient to cover its disclosed total cost.

## 7. Refunds and Reversals

Fees charged in error will be corrected. A properly disclosed fee is not automatically refunded because an instrument later loses value or because the Customer no longer wants the completed service.

## 8. No Hidden Real-World Charges

Alta Terminal will not charge real-world money unless a separate checkout clearly identifies the amount, currency, recipient, refund terms, and service before payment. This schedule does not authorize such a charge.
